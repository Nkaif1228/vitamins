Vitamin B refers to a group of eight essential nutrients that play vital roles in maintaining good health and well-being. These vitamins are water-soluble, meaning they need to be replenished regularly through diet because they are not stored in the body.

There are eight types of B vitamins.

Vitamin B1 (Thiamine):

  • Helps the body convert carbohydrates into energy and is important for the nervous system, muscles, and heart.

    • Source:

    sources include whole grains like brown rice and oatmeal, beans such as black and navy beans, and nuts like sunflower seeds.

    Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin):

  • Involved in energy production and the metabolism of fats, drugs, and steroids. It also helps maintain healthy skin and eyes.

    • Source:

    abundant in dairy products such as milk, cheese, and yogurt, as well as eggs, lean meats, green leafy vegetables like spinach, and almonds.

    Vitamin B3 (Niacin):

  • Supports the nervous system, digestive system, and skin health. It's also involved in energy production and helps lower cholesterol levels.

    • Source:

    can be obtained from poultry, fish like tuna and salmon, lean meats, whole grains, and legumes such as peanuts and lentils.

    Vitamin B5 Pantothenic Acid:

  • Essential for the synthesis of coenzyme A, which is crucial for energy production and the synthesis of hormones

    • Source:

    found in chicken, turkey, avocados, sweet potatoes, sunflower seeds, broccoli, eggs, and whole grains.

    Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine):

  • Vital for amino acid metabolism, red blood cell production, and the creation of neurotransmitters.

    • Source:

    present in poultry, fish, potatoes (especially with skins), bananas, chickpeas, fortified cereals, spinach, and carrots

    Vitamin B7 (Biotin):

  • Important for hair, skin, and nail health. It also plays a role in the metabolism of fatty acids, amino acids, and glucose.

    • Source:

    available in egg yolks, nuts, seeds, legumes, whole grains, cauliflower, sweet potatoes, and avocados

    BVitamin B9 (Folate or Folic Acid):

  • Necessary for DNA synthesis, cell division, and is especially important during pregnancy for fetal development.

    • Source:

    rich sources include leafy green vegetables like spinach and kale, legumes such as lentils and chickpeas, citrus fruits, fortified cereals, beets, Brussels sprouts, and avocados.

    Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin):

  • Vital for red blood cell formation, neurological function, and DNA synthesis.

    • Source:

    primarily found in animal products, including meat, fish, poultry, eggs, and dairy, along with fortified plant-based foods like milk alternatives and cereals. Since Vitamin B12 is mainly found in animal products, those on plant-based diets may need fortified foods or supplements to meet their needs.
